Pixar’s Light and Magic Talk

November 1st | 5:15PM-7:00PM | HUM 115

Christophe Hery joined Pixar in June 2010, where he holds the position of Senior Scientist. After writing new lighting models and rendering methods for Monsters University and The Blue Umbrella. Christophe's latest work includes Finding Dory.

An alumnus of Industrial Light and Magic, Christophe previously served as a research and development lead, supporting the facility's shaders and providing rendering guidance. He was first hired by ILM in 1993 as a senior technical director. During his career at ILM, he received two Technical Achievement Awards from the Academy of Motion Pictures Arts and Sciences.

Christophe will explain to us how maths and physics play a role in shaping the look of some of your favourite movies. In particular, he will focus on various state of the art computer graphics techniques and algorithms, among them subsurface scattering for skin and Monte-Carlo integration for realistic materials and lights.
